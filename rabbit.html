1. Basic Care

Essential daily routines and housing requirements for your rabbit

⏰

Daily Routines

  • Hay: Unlimited timothy hay available 24/7
  • Water: Fresh water daily in heavy ceramic bowl or sipper bottle
  • Litter Box: Spot clean daily, full clean weekly
  • Exercise: 4+ hours supervised free-roam daily
  • Grooming: Brush 2-3 times weekly, check nails monthly
🏠

Housing Requirements

  • Minimum Space: 4ft x 2ft x 2ft enclosure + free-roam time
  • Indoor Only: Protected from drafts, extreme temperatures
  • Hide Box: Small box for security and burrowing instinct
  • Chew Toys: Untreated wood blocks, cardboard tunnels
  • Rabbit-Proofing: Cords covered, baseboards protected
📋

Essential Supplies Checklist

✓ Unlimited timothy hay

✓ Heavy ceramic food/water bowls

✓ Pellet bowl

✓ Litter box with safe litter

✓ Chew toys & tunnels

✓ Nail clippers

✓ Brush/shedding blade

✓ Carrier for vet visits

✓ Rabbit-safe hide box

✓ ID tag or microchip

2. Nutrition

Feeding guidelines and dietary requirements for optimal health

üçÉ

Diet Overview

Rabbits are herbivores requiring 80-90% hay. Feed unlimited timothy hay, limited pellets (1/4 cup per 5lbs body weight daily), and 1 cup fresh veggies per 2lbs body weight.

  • HAY: 80-90% of diet - timothy/ meadow grass hay
  • PELLETS: 5% of diet - high fiber, low calcium
  • VEGETABLES: 10-15% - leafy greens daily
  • FRUIT: Treat only - 1 tsp per 2lbs body weight
⚠️

Foods to Avoid

  • TOXIC: Iceberg lettuce, avocado, chocolate, onion, garlic
  • NO SEEDS/NUTS: High fat causes GI stasis
  • LIMIT STARCH: Bread, pasta, rice, cereal
  • NO DAIRY: Rabbits are lactose intolerant
  • CAREFUL GREENS: Spinach, kale (high calcium) - 1-2x weekly max
💧

Special Dietary Needs

  • Hydration: 50-100ml water per kg body weight daily
  • GI Health: Hay must be longer than rabbit's ear
  • Dental Health: Constant hay chewing prevents overgrown teeth
  • Obesity Prevention: No muesli mixes or unlimited pellets
  • Senior Rabbits: Oxbow senior nutrition pellets

3. Health & Grooming

Preventive care and grooming essentials

🏥

Common Health Issues

  • GI Stasis: No eating/pooping - emergency!
  • Dental Disease: Malocclusion from poor diet
  • Urinary Stones: High calcium diet
  • Ear Mites: Head shaking, scratching
  • Abscesses: From wounds, dental issues
💉

Vaccination Schedule

Kits (8-12 weeks):

  • Myxomatosis: First vaccine + booster 4-6 weeks later
  • RHDV1/RHDV2: Combined vaccine annually

Adults: Annual boosters

ü™í

Grooming Tips

  • Brushing: 2-3x weekly (daily during molt)
  • Nail Trimming: Every 4-6 weeks
  • Bathing: Never - spot clean if needed
  • Fur Blockage: Feed papaya/pineapple tablets during molt
  • Check Bottom: Weekly for urine scald, flystrike prevention

4. Behavior & Training

Understanding and training your bunny companion

üê∞

Understanding Behavior

Rabbits communicate through body language and vocalizations.

  • Binkying: Joyful leaps and twists
  • Flopping: Complete relaxation and trust
  • Thumping: Warning of danger
  • Chinning: Scent marking territory
  • Grinding Teeth: Loud = discomfort, soft = content
🎓

Training Tips

  • Litter Training: Natural instinct - use hay in litter box
  • Positive Reinforcement: Treats (tiny pieces), praise, petting
  • Clicker Training: Mark behaviors with click + treat
  • Carrier Training: Make positive association with treats
  • Handling: Support body fully, never pick up by scruff
🎮

Enrichment Ideas

  • Foraging Toys: Scatter hay, hide pellets in boxes
  • Tunnels & Dens: Cardboard boxes, PVC pipes
  • Chew Variety: Apple wood, willow balls, seagrass mats
  • Companion Rabbit: Spayed/neutered pair ideal
  • Exercise Area: Bunny-proof room with obstacles

5. Emergency Care

Recognizing warning signs and knowing when to seek help

>🚨

Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Care

  • No eating or pooping for 12+ hours
  • Labored breathing or gasping
  • Seizures or collapse
  • Bleeding that won't stop
  • Swollen genitals or straining to urinate
  • Head tilting or circling
  • Flystrike (maggots on bottom)
  • Heatstroke (panting, lethargy)
📞

What to Do in Emergencies

  • Cover Eyes: Reduces stress during transport
  • Warm Towel: For shock - wrap gently
  • Syringe Feeding: Critical care formula if vet advises
  • Massage: Gently on belly clockwise if no poops
  • Call Vet First: Many things hide quickly
ü©∫

Routine Vet Visits

Schedule for:

  • Annual exam + vaccines
  • Weight loss/gain
  • Reduced appetite or poop changes
  • Hair loss or skin issues
  • Lameness or abnormal gait
  • Eye/nose discharge
  • Spay/neuter at 4-6 months

6. Fun Facts & Stories

Amazing facts and heartwarming success stories

🎉

Interesting Facts About Rabbits

  • Rabbits can jump 3ft high and 10ft horizontally
  • They have nearly 360¬∞ vision
  • 30+ breeds recognized worldwide
  • Teeth grow 3mm weekly - must chew constantly
  • Hear ultrasonic frequencies up to 49kHz
  • Oldest rabbit lived 12+ years
  • Recognize their names and up to 50 words
❤️

Real-Life Success Stories

Bonded pairs live longer, happier lives than singles. Many senior rabbits thrive for years in loving homes after being surrendered.

Rescued rabbits from hoarding situations often transform dramatically, becoming affectionate once trust is earned.

Spaying/neutering eliminates 90% of aggression and spraying, making rabbits excellent indoor companions.
