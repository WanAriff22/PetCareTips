1. Basic Care

Essential daily routines and housing requirements for your cat

⏰

Daily Routines

  • Feeding: Two meals approximately 8-12 hours apart (7:00 AM & 7:00 PM)
  • Water: Fresh, clean water available at all times; clean bowl daily
  • Litter Box: Scoop at least once daily to maintain hygiene
  • Playtime: 15 minutes of interactive play twice daily (morning & evening)
  • Grooming: Brush teeth daily and provide regular coat brushing
🏠

Housing Requirements

  • Litter Box: Quiet, accessible location away from food and water
  • Scratching Posts: Multiple surfaces to protect furniture
  • Safe Spaces: Cozy hiding spots and elevated perches
  • Cat-Proofing: Remove toxic plants, secure windows, eliminate choking hazards
📋

Essential Supplies Checklist

✓ Food and water bowls (ceramic/stainless steel)

✓ High-quality age-appropriate cat food

✓ Litter box and cat litter

✓ Scratching post or pad

✓ Cat carrier for vet visits

✓ Brush or comb for grooming

✓ Nail clippers

✓ Toys for mental stimulation

✓ Comfortable bed or blanket

✓ ID collar with contact info

2. Nutrition

Feeding guidelines and dietary requirements for optimal health

🍽️

Diet Overview

Cats are obligate carnivores requiring a protein-rich diet. Feed adult cats twice daily at consistent times, with portions based on weight, age, and activity level.

  • Kittens: 3-4 times daily
  • Adults: Twice daily
  • Seniors: Specially formulated food
⚠️

Foods to Avoid

  • Toxic Foods: Onions, garlic, chives, chocolate, grapes, raisins, xylitol
  • Dairy Products: Most adult cats are lactose intolerant
  • Raw Foods: Raw eggs, meat, fish (harmful bacteria)
  • Bones: Cooked bones can splinter
  • Alcohol & Caffeine: Dangerous even in small amounts
💧

Special Dietary Needs

  • Hydration: Consider wet food to increase moisture intake
  • Weight Management: Monitor portions to prevent obesity (affects 50%+ of cats)
  • Life Stage Nutrition: Age-appropriate formulas (kitten/adult/senior)
  • Medical Conditions: Specialized diets for kidney disease, diabetes, allergies

3. Health & Grooming

Preventive care and grooming essentials

🏥

Common Health Issues

  • Dental Disease: Brush teeth daily, schedule professional cleanings
  • Obesity: Control portions and encourage daily exercise
  • Urinary Tract Issues: Fresh water and clean litter boxes
  • Parasites: Monthly flea, tick, and worm prevention
  • Hairballs: Regular brushing reduces ingested fur
💉

Vaccination Schedule

Kittens: Starting at 8-9 weeks

  • FVRCP: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks, then boosters
  • Rabies: At 12-16 weeks, boosters as required by law
  • FeLV: Recommended for outdoor cats

Adults: Boosters annually or every 3 years

✂️

Grooming Tips

  • Brushing: Weekly for short-hair, daily for long-hair breeds
  • Nail Trimming: Every 2-3 weeks with cat-specific clippers
  • Bathing: Generally unnecessary; cats self-groom
  • Ear Cleaning: Check weekly for dirt, wax, or infection
  • Eye Care: Gently wipe discharge with damp cloth

4. Behavior & Training

Understanding and training your feline companion

🐾

Understanding Behavior

Cats communicate through body language, vocalizations, and scent marking.

  • Relaxed: Forward ears, soft eyes, gently swaying tail
  • Fearful: Flattened ears, dilated pupils, puffed tail
  • Scratching: Territory marking and claw maintenance
  • Kneading: Shows contentment
🎓

Training Tips

  • Litter Box: Most kittens use instinctively; show location and keep clean
  • Positive Reinforcement: Reward immediately with treats, praise, or play
  • Clicker Training: Mark correct behaviors followed by rewards
  • Discourage Bad Behavior: Never punish physically; redirect to alternatives
  • Consistency: Use same commands among all family members
🎮

Enrichment Ideas

  • Interactive Toys: Rotate regularly to maintain interest
  • Puzzle Feeders: Encourage natural foraging behaviors
  • Window Perches: Safe outdoor observation
  • Vertical Space: Cat trees or shelves for climbing
  • Play Sessions: Regular interactive play with wand toys
  • Companionship: Consider a second cat if yours enjoys company

5. Emergency Care

Recognizing warning signs and knowing when to seek help

🚨

Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Care

  • Difficulty breathing (gasping, open-mouth breathing)
  • Collapse or unconsciousness
  • Severe injuries (deep wounds, broken bones, excessive bleeding)
  • Seizures (convulsions, tremors)
  • Inability to urinate (especially male cats)
  • Toxic ingestion (poisons, human medications)
  • Severe vomiting or diarrhea (with blood or lasting 24+ hours)
  • Eye injuries (sudden blindness, bulging eyes)
📞

What to Do in Emergencies

  • Stay Calm: Your anxiety stresses your cat
  • Call Ahead: Contact emergency vet before arriving
  • Safe Transport: Use secure carrier; injured cats may bite
  • Don't Feed: Avoid food/water if surgery might be needed
  • Bring Records: Vaccination records and medication list
📅

When to See a Vet

Schedule non-emergency appointments for:

  • Changes in eating, drinking, or litter habits
  • Weight loss or gain
  • Lethargy lasting 24+ hours
  • Persistent coughing or sneezing
  • Limping or difficulty moving
  • Excessive scratching or skin problems
  • Annual wellness checkups and vaccinations

6. Fun Facts & Stories

Amazing facts and heartwarming success stories

Interesting Facts About Cats

  • Cats spend 70% of their lives sleeping (13-16 hours daily)
  • A cat's purr vibrates at 25-150 Hz, promoting healing and reducing stress
  • Cats have 20+ vocalizations, using meow primarily for humans
  • A cat's nose print is unique, like human fingerprints
  • Cats can rotate their ears 180Â° and hear up to 64 kHz
  • The oldest known cat lived to 38 years old
  • Cats have a third eyelid (nictitating membrane) for eye protection
❤️

Real-Life Success Stories

Adoption stories demonstrate the transformative power of giving cats second chances. Many rescue cats initially show fearful or withdrawn behavior but blossom into affectionate companions once they feel safe in their new homes.

Senior cats often make excellent pets for calm households, despite being frequently overlooked at shelters. Special needs cats with conditions like FIV or three legs adapt remarkably well and live full, happy lives with proper care.

Foster-to-adopt programs help shy or traumatized cats decompress in home environments, increasing their chances of successful permanent placement.
