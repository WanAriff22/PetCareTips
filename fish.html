1. Aquarium Setup

Essential water quality and housing requirements for your fish

⏰

Daily Routines

  • Feeding: Once or twice daily (only what they eat in 2 mins)
  • Temperature: Check thermometer daily (76-80Â°F for tropicals)
  • Observation: Count fish and check for signs of illness
  • Lighting: 8-10 hours of light daily (use a timer)
  • Filter Check: Ensure water is flowing properly
🏠

Housing Requirements

  • Tank Size: "Bigger is better" (easier to maintain stability)
  • Filtration: Crucial for removing toxins (Mechanical/Bio/Chemical)
  • Substrate: Gravel or sand depending on species
  • Decor: Plants (live/silk) and caves for hiding spots
📋

Essential Supplies Checklist

✓ Aquarium tank and stand

✓ Filter and replacement media

✓ Heater and thermometer

✓ LED Aquarium lighting

✓ Water conditioner (dechlorinator)

✓ Liquid test kit (Ammonia/Nitrate)

✓ Gravel vacuum / Siphon

✓ Fish net and Algae scraper

✓ Substrate (sand/gravel)

✓ High-quality fish food

2. Nutrition

Feeding guidelines to prevent bloating and water contamination

🍽️

Diet Overview

Fish require a varied diet. Overfeeding is the #1 cause of fish death and water quality issues.

  • Staple: High-quality flakes or pellets
  • Frozen/Live: Bloodworms, brine shrimp (treats)
  • Vegetables: Blanched peas or zucchini (for herbivores)
⚠️

Common Mistakes

  • Overfeeding: Uneaten food rots and spikes ammonia
  • Wrong Food Size: Pellets too big for small mouths
  • Old Food: Vitamins degrade after opening (replace every 6 mos)
  • Human Food: Bread and crackers can be fatal
  • Holiday Feeders: Block feeders often pollute the water
💧

Dietary Types

  • Carnivores: Need high protein (Bettas, Cichlids)
  • Herbivores: Need algae wafers/veggies (Plecos, Mollies)
  • Omnivores: Need a mix of both (Goldfish, Tetras)
  • Bottom Feeders: Sinking pellets required

3. Health & Maintenance

Water chemistry and disease prevention

🏥

Common Diseases

  • Ich (White Spot): Parasite looking like salt grains
  • Fin Rot: Fraying fins due to poor water quality
  • Swim Bladder: Floating upside down/sinking (constipation)
  • Dropsy: Pinecone-like scales (kidney failure)
  • Fungal: Cotton-like growths on body
💉

The Nitrogen Cycle

The most important concept in fish keeping:

  • Ammonia: Fish waste (Toxic) → Bacteria converts it
  • Nitrite: Converted waste (Toxic) → Bacteria converts it
  • Nitrate: Final stage (Less toxic) → Remove via water changes
  • Note: Never scrub filter media in tap water (kills bacteria)
✂️

Maintenance Schedule

  • Weekly: 15-25% water change with siphon
  • Weekly: Test water parameters (pH, Ammonia, Nitrite)
  • Monthly: Rinse filter sponge in tank water only
  • Monthly: Clean algae off glass
  • As needed: Prune live plants

4. Behavior & Compatibility

Understanding fish social structures and tank mates

🐾

Understanding Behavior

Fish behavior indicates health and stress levels.

  • Shoaling: Tetras need groups of 6+ to feel safe
  • Surface Gasping: Low oxygen levels (Emergency)
  • Rubbing (Flashing): Parasites or skin irritation
  • Hiding: Stress, bullying, or illness
🎓

Compatibility Tips

  • Community Fish: Peaceful (Guppies, Mollies, Tetras)
  • Semi-Aggressive: Fin nippers (Tiger Barbs, some Gouramis)
  • Aggressive: Territory defenders (Cichlids, Male Bettas)
  • Rule of Thumb: If a fish fits in another's mouth, it will be eaten
  • Temperature: Don't mix Goldfish (Cold) with Tropicals (Warm)
🎮

Enrichment Ideas

  • Varied Environment: Rocks, driftwood, and caves
  • Current: Powerheads for river-loving species
  • Live Food: Encourages natural hunting instincts
  • Rearranging: Change decor during cleaning to reduce territorial aggression
  • Training: Goldfish/Bettas can learn to follow fingers for food

5. Emergency Care

Recognizing warning signs and acting fast

🚨

Warning Signs - Act Immediately

  • Fish lying on the bottom or floating sideways
  • Gasping at surface (indicates ammonia spike or low oxygen)
  • White spots covering body (Ich outbreak)
  • Cloudy eyes or popping eyes
  • Visible open sores or red streaks
  • Sudden mass death in the tank
  • Cracked tank or leaking filter
📞

What to Do in Emergencies

  • Test Water First: 90% of issues are water quality related
  • Water Change: Perform immediate 30-50% water change
  • Quarantine: Move sick fish to a hospital tank
  • Check Equipment: Ensure heater and filter are running
  • Stop Feeding: Do not feed until issue resolves
📅

Hospital Tank Setup

Keep a spare small tank ready:

  • Bare bottom (no gravel) for easy cleaning
  • Simple sponge filter
  • Heater
  • No carbon in filter (it removes medication)
  • Use for treating sick fish or quarantining new arrivals

6. Fun Facts

Amazing facts about the underwater world

🎉

Interesting Facts About Fish

  • Goldfish have a memory span of at least 3 months, not 3 seconds
  • Fish have a "Lateral Line" organ to detect vibration and movement
  • Some fish (like Bettas) can breathe air from the surface (Labyrinth organ)
  • Fish do sleep, but they don't have eyelids to close
  • There are over 30,000 known species of fish
  • Clownfish can change their gender
  • The oldest pet Goldfish lived to be 43 years old
❤️

Aquarium Therapy

Studies show that watching an aquarium can significantly reduce stress and lower blood pressure.

This is why you often see fish tanks in dentist offices and hospitals. The slow, rhythmic movement of the fish and the sound of bubbling water have a hypnotic, calming effect on the human brain.

Maintaining a planted tank (aquascaping) is also considered a form of artistic expression and meditation for many hobbyists.
