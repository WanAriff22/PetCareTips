Getting Started with Pet Care

Essential information for new and experienced pet owners

🍖

Nutrition Basics

  • Balanced Diet: Species-appropriate food for optimal health
  • Fresh Water: Clean water available at all times
  • Portion Control: Proper serving sizes to prevent obesity
  • Feeding Schedule: Consistent meal times for better digestion
  • Treats: Limited to 10% of daily caloric intake
🏠

Housing Requirements

  • Safe Space: Comfortable area for rest and sleep
  • Temperature: Proper climate control year-round
  • Cleanliness: Regular cleaning of living areas
  • Enrichment: Toys and activities for mental stimulation
  • Pet-Proofing: Remove hazards and toxic materials
💖

Daily Care Routine

  • Morning: Feeding, fresh water, and quick health check
  • Exercise: Physical activity based on species needs
  • Playtime: Interactive engagement and bonding
  • Grooming: Regular brushing and hygiene maintenance
  • Evening: Second feeding and quality time together

Health & Emergency Preparedness

Be ready for any situation with your pet

💨

Emergency Kit Must-Haves

  • Medical records and vaccination history
  • First aid supplies and medications
  • 3-7 days supply of food and water
  • Carriers and restraints for transport
  • Recent photos for identification
  • Emergency contact numbers
⚠

Warning Signs to Watch

  • Breathing Issues: Rapid or labored breathing
  • Lethargy: Unusual tiredness or weakness
  • Loss of Appetite: Not eating for 24+ hours
  • Digestive Problems: Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • Behavior Changes: Aggression or hiding
  • Physical Injuries: Bleeding or limping
💉

Preventive Health Care

  • Regular Checkups: Annual veterinary examinations
  • Vaccinations: Keep immunizations current
  • Parasite Control: Prevent fleas, ticks, and worms
  • Dental Care: Daily teeth brushing when possible
  • Weight Management: Monitor body condition regularly
  • Grooming: Regular coat and nail maintenance

Foods to Avoid

Common items that are toxic to pets

❌

Highly Toxic Foods

  • Chocolate: Contains theobromine - can be fatal
  • Grapes & Raisins: Cause kidney failure in dogs
  • Onions & Garlic: Damage red blood cells
  • Xylitol: Artificial sweetener causing liver failure
  • Alcohol: Extremely dangerous to all pets
  • Caffeine: Can cause heart problems
⛔

Other Dangerous Items

  • Avocado: Contains persin - toxic to birds
  • Macadamia Nuts: Cause weakness in dogs
  • Cooked Bones: Splintering hazard
  • Raw Dough: Expands in stomach
  • Salt: Large amounts cause sodium poisoning
  • Certain Plants: Lilies, azaleas, and more
✅

Safe Treat Alternatives

  • Carrots: Crunchy and low-calorie
  • Apples: Remove seeds and core first
  • Blueberries: Antioxidant-rich treats
  • Plain Chicken: Cooked, unseasoned meat
  • Sweet Potato: Cooked and mashed
  • Green Beans: Plain, cooked or raw
