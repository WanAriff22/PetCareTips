1. Housing & Heat

Essential enclosure setup, lighting, and temperature requirements

☀️

Lighting & Heat

  • UVB Lamp: Mandatory for shell strength (Replace every 6-12 months)
  • Basking Spot: 95-100°F (35-38°C) needed for digestion
  • Cool Zone: 70-75°F (21-24°C) to allow thermoregulation
  • Night Heat: Use ceramic heat emitter (CHE) if temp drops below 65°F
🏠

Enclosure Requirements

  • Type: "Tortoise Table" (open top) usually better than glass tanks
  • Size: Minimum 4ft x 2ft for small species; larger for Sulcatas
  • Substrate: Coco coir, cypress mulch, or topsoil (holds humidity)
  • Hides: Provide humid hides to prevent shell pyramiding
  • Water: Shallow dish for soaking and drinking
📋

Essential Supplies Checklist

✓ Tortoise Table / Large Enclosure

✓ UVB Tube Light (T5 or T8)

✓ Basking Heat Lamp

✓ Ceramic Heat Emitter (Night)

✓ Calcium Powder (with D3)

✓ Thermometer & Hygrometer

✓ Shallow water ramp bowl

✓ Slate rock (for eating off)

✓ Substrate (Soil/Coco Coir)

✓ Cuttlefish bone

✓ Hide box / Cave

2. Nutrition

High fiber diets for slow growth and smooth shells

🌿

Diet Overview

Most tortoises are herbivores requiring a high-fiber, low-protein, low-sugar diet.

  • Staple (80%): Weeds, grasses, hay, dandelion, hibiscus
  • Greens: Kale, collard greens, mustard greens
  • Supplements: Sprinkle calcium on food 2-3 times a week
  • Water: Soak tortoise 2-3 times weekly for 15 mins
⚠️

Foods to Avoid

  • Fruit: Too much sugar causes gut problems (except for Red-foots)
  • Iceberg Lettuce: No nutritional value
  • Animal Protein: Causes shell deformities and kidney failure
  • Toxic Plants: Azalea, Daffodil, Rhubarb leaves, Avocado
  • Dog/Cat Food: Never feed this to tortoises
💧

Species Variations

  • Grazers (Sulcata, Leopard): Need 70%+ grass and hay
  • Mediterranean (Hermann's, Horsefield): Weeds and leafy greens
  • Tropical (Red-foot): Can have small amounts of fruit and higher humidity

3. Health & Shell Care

Preventing pyramiding and maintaining beak health

🏥

Common Health Issues

  • Pyramiding: bumpy shell caused by dry air, lack of UVB, or high protein
  • Respiratory Infection: Runny nose, bubbly eyes (too cold/damp)
  • Metabolic Bone Disease: Soft shell due to lack of Calcium/UVB
  • Beak Overgrowth: Needs trimming if not eating on slate/rocks
  • Parasites: Worms (check fecal sample annually)
🛁

Soaking Routine

Hydration is key. Tortoises absorb water through their cloaca.

  • Frequency: Babies daily; Adults 2-3 times/week
  • Depth: Up to the chin (do not submerge head)
  • Temp: Lukewarm (baby bath temperature)
  • Duration: 15-20 minutes
✂️

Grooming Tips

  • Beak: Feed on a slate rock to naturally file down the beak
  • Nails: Usually wear down naturally on rocks; vet can trim
  • Shell: Gently brush with a soft toothbrush during baths
  • Oil: NEVER put oil on the shell (blocks pores/heat absorption)

4. Behavior

Understanding your tortoise's personality

🐾

Typical Behaviors

  • Basking: Stretching out legs under the heat lamp
  • Burrowing: Digging into substrate to sleep or cool down
  • Head Bobbing: Mating ritual or dominance display
  • Ramming: Hitting shell against objects/others (aggression)
🎓

Handling Tips

  • Support: Always hold with two hands supporting the bottom
  • Stress: They stress easily; limit handling time
  • Flipping: If they flip on their back, help them immediately
  • Outdoor Time: Great for natural UVB, but supervise closely!
🎮

Enrichment

  • Texture: Vary substrate with flat rocks, soil, and mulch
  • Climbing: Safe, low obstacles (hills, flat logs)
  • Foraging: Hide food around the enclosure
  • Plants: Edible live plants (Spider plant, Aloe) to graze on

5. Emergency Care

When to seek a reptile vet

🚨

Warning Signs - Seek Care

  • Bubbles coming from nose or mouth
  • Wheezing or gasping for air
  • Soft shell (in adults)
  • Swollen eyes or inability to open eyes
  • Not eating for more than 3-4 days (if warm)
  • Prolapse (tissue protruding from tail area)
  • Cracked shell from a fall or dog bite
📞

Emergency Actions

  • Warmth: Keep the tortoise warm (85°F) while transporting
  • Shell Crack: Cover with sterile gauze; do not apply creams
  • Find a Specialist: Regular dog/cat vets often don't treat reptiles
  • Records: Bring photos of your enclosure setup to the vet
📅

Hibernation

Note: Not all species hibernate!

  • Do Not Hibernate: Tropical species (Sulcata, Leopard, Red-foot)
  • Can Hibernate: Mediterranean species (Hermann's, Horsefield)
  • Warning: Only healthy tortoises should hibernate. Consult a vet first.

6. Fun Facts

Amazing facts about these ancient reptiles

🎉

Tortoise Trivia

  • Tortoises can feel when you touch their shell (it has nerve endings!)
  • They have been around for over 200 million years (older than snakes)
  • Giant tortoises can live over 150 years; pets often outlive their owners
  • They cannot swim (unlike turtles) and will drown in deep water
  • A group of tortoises is called a "creep"
  • They inhale by moving their neck and legs, as their ribs are fused to the shell
❤️

A Lifetime Commitment

Adopting a tortoise is often a multi-generational commitment. Many tortoises are handed down in wills!

Despite their slow reputation, they can be surprisingly fast and are known to be escape artists. They are intelligent creatures that can recognize their owners and will often come running for food (or a head scratch).

Rescue tortoises are often available because people underestimate their lifespan or size.
